LIFE AFTER DEATH

If there were one map to life and beyond, would you follow it?

A short, careful walk through Scripture that explains our need, God's rescue, and how to respond. Read slowly — let the verses speak.

Scroll down to continue. Each step will appear as you reach it.

STEP 1 — OUR CONDITION

We are separated from God

Romans 3:23 — "For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."

Sin is not just bad choices — it is a state of separation from the life God intends. Think: white lies, cheating, habitual selfishness — they show we are not perfect.

STEP 2 — THE CONSEQUENCE

Sin has a cost

Romans 6:23a — "For the wages of sin is death..."

Death here speaks of separation — the ultimate consequence of choosing against the life of God. Like bad choices that lead to worse outcomes, sin leads to a spiritual end without rescue.

STEP 3 — GOD'S OFFER

But there is a gift

Romans 6:23b — "...but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord."

This is not earned. It is offered. The bridge across our separation is God's grace — free, undeserved, and received by faith.

STEP 4 — THE CROSS

Jesus took our place

Romans 5:8 — "But God demonstrates His own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us."

God's love is active — it doesn't wait for us to become perfect. Jesus entered our mess to bring forgiveness and restoration.

STEP 5 — PERSONAL RESPONSE

Believe. Confess. Receive.

Romans 10:9 — "If you confess with your mouth, 'Jesus is Lord,' and believe in your heart that God raised Him from the dead, you will be saved."

Salvation is both belief and confession — an inward faith and outward acknowledgment. It's a personal decision: will you trust the map God provides?

STEP 6 — THE PROMISE

Secure in His love

Romans 8:38–39 — "For I am convinced that neither death nor life... will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord."

Once received, the relationship is real. The Christian life moves forward in trust, community, growth, and the hope of resurrection.

INVITATION

Would you like to follow today?

If you want to respond, try this simple prayer in your own words. If you'd like guidance, consider talking with a pastor or a mature believer.

"Lord Jesus, I know I'm separated from You. I believe You died and rose for me. I receive Your gift of forgiveness and new life. Help me follow You. Amen."

Whether you prayed or are still thinking, take a moment: write down what this step would mean for your life, or speak to someone you trust.
